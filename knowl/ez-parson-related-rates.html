Checkpoint 1.2.1. Solving a Related Rates Problem.

A water tank has the shape of an inverted circular cone (point down) with a base of radius 6 feet and a depth of 8 feet. Suppose that water is being pumped into the tank at a constant instantaneous rate of 4 cubic feet per minute.

Draw a picture of the conical tank, including a sketch of the water level at a point in time when the tank is not yet full.

---

Let \(r\) be the radius and \(h\) the depth of the water at a given time, \(t\text{.}\)

---

Notice that \(h=(4/3)r\text{.}\)

---

Notice that \(r=6\) and \(h=8\text{.}\)
 #paired
---

Notice that \((1/3)\pi r^2 h\text{.}\)
 #paired
---

We know that at time \(t\text{,}\) \(V=(1/3)\pi r^2h\text{.}\)

---

We know that \(V=(1/3)\pi 6^2\cdot 8 = 96\pi\text{.}\)
 #paired
---

Differentiate and obtain \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2\frac{dr}{dt}\text{.}\)

---

Differentiate and obtain \(\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{2}{3}\pi rh\frac{dr}{dt}\text{.}\)
 #paired
---

Differentiate and obtain \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2\text{.}\)
 #paired
---

Substitute \(h=(4/3)r\text{,}\) giving \(V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\text{.}\)
